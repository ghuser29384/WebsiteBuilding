Moral Cognitivism vs. Non-Cognitivism

Explore how metaethical theories connect, conflict, and depend on deeper assumptions about truth, motivation, and moral explanation.

Core Contrast

Moral Cognitivism

Moral judgments express beliefs and are truth-apt. Theories differ on whether any moral beliefs are actually true.

Moral Non-Cognitivism

Moral judgments primarily express non-belief attitudes, prescriptions, or commitments rather than ordinary truth-evaluable beliefs.

Undergirding Issues

  • Truthmaker Maximalism and whether every truth requires a robust ontological ground.
  • The queerness challenge to objective moral properties.
  • Frege-Geach style embedding problems for expressivist semantics.
  • Motivation internalism and whether sincere moral judgment necessarily motivates.
  • Moral disagreement: belief conflict, attitude clash, or both.
